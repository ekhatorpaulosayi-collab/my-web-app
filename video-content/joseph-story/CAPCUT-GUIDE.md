## STEP 4: Add Voiceover (5 minutes)

## STEP 5: Add Music (2 minutes)

1. Drag music track to audio layer
2. Adjust volume: 20-30% (voiceover should be louder)
3. Add fade in/out at start/end (1 second each)
4. Cut music to end at 0:30

**Good free music sources:**
- CapCut's built-in library (filter by "Upbeat")
- YouTube Audio Library
- Epidemic Sound (if you have subscription)

---

## STEP 6: Captions (3 minutes)

**Easy way:**
1. Click "Text" ‚Üí "Auto Captions"
2. Select language: English
3. CapCut generates captions automatically
4. Review and fix any errors

**Manual way:**
1. Import captions.srt file
2. CapCut will add all subtitles automatically

**Caption style:**
- Font: Montserrat Bold
- Size: 70
- Color: White with black stroke
- Position: Center or bottom third
- Background: Semi-transparent black box (optional)

## TROUBLESHOOTING

**Text is too small:**
- Increase font size to 100+
- Remember people watch on phones!

**Voiceover is quiet:**
- Select audio clip
- Click "Volume"
- Increase to 150-200%

**Music is too loud:**
- Select music clip
- Reduce volume to 20-30%
- Voiceover should always be louder

**Video feels slow:**
- Speed up clips to 1.2x
- Cut 1-2 seconds from less important scenes
- Make transitions faster (0.1s instead of 0.2s)

**Captions don't sync:**
- Use "Auto Captions" feature
- OR manually adjust timing in timeline

---

## KEYBOARD SHORTCUTS (Speed up editing)

- **Space**: Play/Pause
- **S**: Split clip at playhead
- **Delete**: Remove selected clip
- **Ctrl+Z**: Undo
- **Ctrl+D**: Duplicate clip
- **+/-**: Zoom in/out timeline
